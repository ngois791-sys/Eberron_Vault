The Chronicle · Arc 4

Arc 4 — The Fount of Inarieth

Session 17 Session 18 Session 19 Session 20 Interlude 4 Session 21

Session 17

Last time, you left Arcanix for Gatherhold and the Talenta Plains. Ziolda replaced the haft of her glaive with the strange rod you got from Sora Teraza. Leroy saw some missing person posters for a man named Brian Hostar and got weirdly quiet after that. Then when Ziolda tried to get Kitt and Flynt time alone, Kitt gave Flynt finger guns and took off.

You briefly stopped in Gatherhold where Hulrik told you you'd be welcome any time in his home in the Mror Holds before he took off. Leroy looked out across Lake Cyre and saw a warforged emerge from the mists riding a mechanical beast and you inferred that warforged were unaffected by the Mourning.

You then headed for Kitt's tribe to see Weyla. When you got there, you heard Weyla arguing with her sister, Welda, who had found Weyla's druidic books and was going to inform the tribe. Welda called Kitt a freak and then left. When she saw you all outside Weyla's house, she spat at Kitt's feet and tried to walk off, but Ziolda tripped her.

You suggested to Weyla that she should leave the tribe and go north to meet Sylvari, your druid friend in Lakeside. Then Leroy loosed Oswald on the tribe along with several geese summoned by Kitt. You all quickly got out of there before the repercussions of those actions could become clear and headed out into the Blade Desert toward Q'barra.

While crossing the desert, you briefly got caught in flooding slot canyons and then found shelter in the remains of a crashed airship. Inside the wreckage were three pristine Khyber dragonshards.

The next day you saved Peanut from being buried under a collapsing sand dune. Kitt cast a spell through Peanut for the first time, using Thunderwave to blast the sand off him. Then you came across three wandering warforged: Ben, Mel, and Desi. They seemed like they were low on power and their joints were filled with sand. They said they were exiled by the Lord of Blades from the Forge-Sworn Empire, the country established by the warforged who survived The Mourning in Cyre. You gave them each one of the Khyber dragonshards to repower them and sent them to Cardell Keep.

The next day you fought a giant scorpion monster that stung Leroy and Ziolda, preventing them from getting any health from rests. The fourth day you fought through a sandstorm that uncovered some ancient relics from a war between Valenar elves and goblins. Many of the goblins had symbols of The Great Demon on them and one of the elves had a suit of chitinous armor. Ziolda put the armor on and was knocked out by a stinger inside it that injected poison into her. Once she recovered, though, she felt the stinger still there but giving her more alertness and protecting her from the heat.

You came across some nomads who told you that the stinger of the scorpion creature, a skriton, would continue to harm Leroy and Ziolda unless the barbs left inside them were surgically removed. Ziolda had to take the armor back off to get the surgery. After getting the stinger barbs removed by the nomads, you headed into the tunnel they said would lead you through the Endworld Mountains. The same tunnel the Order of the Emerald Claw took. But the nomads warned you that monsters lurk in those tunnels and their tribe avoids them whenever possible.

You entered the tunnels and found the body of one of the Order's members. Then you heard a horrible howling noise echoing through the tunnels that sent chills down your spines and felt like it was echoing in your very brains.

Session 18

Last time, you started off with a fight against a howler. You defeated it easily, but heard more approaching in the distance, drawn toward the howls of the one you just killed. You decided to make a run for it through the tunnels of the Endworld Mountains that would lead you to Q'barra.

You came to a fork in the tunnels and opted for a route that took you to a large cavern containing the ruins of an ancient dwarven city and the remains of a battle between duergar and mountains dwarves. As you took shelter to hide from the pursuing howlers, you ended up in a room where the last king of these mountain dwarves, Bromdahr Steelfist made his last stand.

Kitt took the mithral crown inlaid with emeralds from the dwarf king's skeleton and placed it on her head. Leroy took the king's glammerweave cloak, which is embroidered with the royal lineage and is capable of showing images of events that took place in proximity to the cloak. From the cloak, you learned that the war between the factions of dwarves ended, but the survivors were destroyed by an ancient deep dragon living in the tunnels.

You also found a strange diamond with a circular depression in the top. The depression is surrounded by a ring of dwarven runes which you couldn't read.

After the howlers moved on, you found evidence that the Order of the Emerald Claw passed through this way and followed the tracks out to the other side of the mountains. You emerged on cliffs overlooking a dense jungle. As you climbed down into the jungle, you encountered Rott and Ragweed, kobold monks afflicted with extreme stench and extreme allergies.

The pair brought you to their monastery, The Sacred Roost, to meet with their grandmaster, Orris, and his second in command, Kinche. You met two more kobolds, Cobalt and Patina, guarding something in the main building and Ziolda cured Rott of whatever affliction made him so smelly. Patina led you to the masters and you learned that Orris had a vision while meditating of hugely important strangers arriving at the monastery.

At first, he thought that the members of the Order of the Emerald Claw were those strangers, but it became clear that they were not friendly and they were sent away. You learned that the monks guard something called the Stele of the Ancestors which bears a mysterious inscription in a dead language brought over the sea by the dragonborn ancestors of the kobolds and the lizardfolk. Inarieth had visited this monastery, drawn to the stele, but she didn't have time to translate it before she was called away again.

You made a copy of the inscription to try to translate as you went to lodgings prepared for you. You spent the evening translating and interpreting the text before going to bed. But you were awoken in the middle of the night by a series of explosions and screams. Flames rose from the monastery as lizardfolk started to invade.

Session 19

Last time, you jumped straight into a fight to save the kobold monastery. Kitt summoned a tidal wave to knock back the invading lizardfolk at the wall, allowing the kobolds to rally their defenses there. While you fought against three of the larger lizardfolk, Leroy sent Oswald to attack others with dynamite.

As you pressed on through the monastery toward the Hall of a Thousand Mirrors, you ran into Cobalt, who helped you fight your way through. When you got to the Hall, you found Grandmaster Orris using all of his ki powers to cling to life just long enough to tell you that the Order of the Emerald Claw took the inscription from the Stele of the Ancestors to help them track down the Fount of Inarieth.

When the lizardfolk had been driven back and the monastery was under control again, Cobalt told you about the sea elf city of Inarion across the Tempest Strait and gave you options for crossing the strait. You chose to head to Newthrone, the lizardfolk capital, to look for a merchant ship willing to make the trip through the Strait.

As you entered town, you saw a strange vehicle called The Wandering Inn, which bore the House Ghallanda crest. Then you thoroughly confused a dockworker before telling him you wanted to cross the Strait. He realized that your confusing behavior must have been because you were insane and directed you to a tavern.

Kitt, as a dog, followed a perfume scent from the Wandering Inn to the same tavern. Inside, you found a halfling bard performing for a rapt audience. After the performance Kitt went up to her for scratches before dashing out of the tavern and changing back into herself.

Meanwhile, Leroy questioned ship captains about crossing the Strait and Ziolda got immediately drunk on something called Kraken's Blood. Leroy was approached by a drow who called himself Captain Shadow. He said that he and his crew on The Liberation could easily sail the Strait and that they had done so several times already.

Kitt chatted with the bard, whose name was Isamira d'Ghallanda, or Izzy for short. She is the granddaughter of the leader of House Ghallanda in Sharn and she volunteered to run the Wandering Inn to get some time alone from her family and go on adventures to interesting places. She made her own modifications to the Inn and hacked its programming to allow her to deviate from the predetermined routes.

Kitt brought up using the Inn to cross the Strait and Izzy excitedly started sketching potential modifications to allow for water travel. You all decided to go with Izzy instead of Shadow and after the modifications were complete, you headed out onto the water.

Izzy told you that she didn't require payment for the trip. Her only request was that you give her some good stories that might inspire her to write some songs.

As you neared Inarion, a storm started to pick up in the Strait and with the flashes of lightning, Ziolda saw a gargantuan dragon made of lightning in the sky over the water. As you got closer, Ziolda felt drawn toward a specific spot at the epicenter of the storm. She dove in, using her Silver Flame armor to weigh her down, and began searching the seafloor.

On the surface, Leroy told Izzy the story of how he lost his family and got revenge by killing the werewolves of the Dark Pack. Ziolda eventually found the sword that had once belonged to Inarieth, which tingled her hand when she touched it.

She brought it back to the surface and you examined it. When it got near Ziolda's glaive, the ivory scales on the glaive started to crackle, and when they made contact, a spark jumped between the weapons, charging the glaive with some sort of lightning magic Ziolda can unleash on enemies.

Ziolda saw a vision of Inarieth fighting the lightning dragon, turning the lightning that struck the sword into a blast of radiance that killed it but disintegrated her body in the process. She sank beneath the waves and Ziolda saw a familiar scene of golden light breaking through the clouds into the water. Then you turned the Inn toward Inarion.

Session 20

Last time, you made it to the ruins of the sea elf city of Inarion and met a sea elf named Inarianna, or just Anna. She told you that the ruins had been taken over by the grung, long-time enemies of the sea elves who moved in when the elves were wiped out by a cataclysm. Anna is a descendant of the paladin Inarieth and, to her knowledge, the last of the sea elves, after the few who escaped the cataclysm gradually died off.

Ziolda gave Anna the sword of Inarieth that she had retrieved from the bottom of the sea. And with Anna, you fought and killed the grung king and drove the remainder of the grung back into the ocean. You then looked around the ruins for information about the Fount of Inarieth.

You found information that led you to the chambers of Priestess Liarora, who was the last one to know the route to the Fount. In her quarters, you found a safe that said only someone with the healing touch of Arawai, the goddess of the sea elves, could open it. Anna, as a cleric of Arawai, tried, but it didn't work. It did work, however, when Ziolda tried. Inside you found a text describing the location of the Fount and how to get there.

You went with Izzy and Anna in the Wandering Inn to Dreadwood Isle and began the three-day-long hike up to the top of the mountain, anticipating a fight with the Order of the Emerald Claw. Out in the Tempest Strait, you saw a large ship anchored and the beach showed signs of people recently arriving in smaller boats. Izzy, not being much of a fighter, stayed in the Wandering Inn, burrowing it down into the sand of the beach to get out of sight of anyone on the ship.

When you reached the top, you found an armored figure with blue light glowing from the eyeholes of his masked helmet along with several Blood of Vol mages. The figure, Lord Soth, was just finishing killing the last of the Emerald Claw knights and the mages were preparing a ritual around the Fount.

When you stepped out into the open, Lord Soth told you that he had been charged by his patron, The Smith, to destroy the Fount. He also said that he had no quarrel with you and there would be no need for a fight if you just let them go about their business.

Of course, you ignored this and attacked. Anna, Peanut, and Kitt went after the mages to disrupt their ritual while Ziolda and Leroy took on Lord Soth. At one point, Leroy had Oswald shove a stick of dynamite down the back of Lord Soth's pants. Then Lord Soth had enough of you and took both Ziolda and Leroy down with a cone of cold before going after Anna. He cut one of her arms clean off before Ziolda and Leroy could rejoin the fight.

You gradually whittled Lord Soth down until he was forced to use a spell to create a portal and escape. Before he disappeared, he looked back at Leroy and said, "This isn't over. I'll be seeing you again." His eyes glowed brighter and Leroy's started to burn as well.

Anna passed out from shock and blood loss, and everyone else finished off the mages, ending the ritual before the Fount could be fully corrupted. You put Anna into the Fount to use its restorative magic to reattach her arm. Everyone else got into the water as well, Ziolda all the way under to speak with Inarieth and Leroy eyes first.

Ziolda saw Inarieth underwater in a fractured form held together by golden magic. She gave Ziolda her gauntlets, which were sucked into Ziolda's pendant like Tira's shield was, and Ziolda now has the ability to magically take damage instead of her nearby allies.

Above the water, Leroy and Kitt experimented with Leroy's new ability to see through objects. Kitt felt more powerfully magical and realized that she's nearing the end of Flynt's spellbook and will have to find another druid to teach her more powerful spells in the future.

You made plans to meet up with Hulrik so he could fly you from Cliffscrape to Regalport, the capital of the Lhazaar Principalities and the city mentioned in the ancient poem you translated.

Interlude 4

Session 21

Last time, you made your way down the mountain from the Fount of Inarieth. Along the way, Kitt experimented with some chaos potions with Leroy as the test subject. At the end of the first day of hiking, Leroy attempted to contact his patron, and Oswald went into the sort of trance that means the Great Dragon is speaking through him. Leroy asked about Lord Soth, if he is also a warlock of the Great Dragon, and why she would send him to destroy the Fount.

The Great Dragon said that Lord Soth is a warlock that has become powerful enough that he doesn't have to rely on her for all of his magic anymore. He has also started to act without the Great Dragon's directions. She told him to go destroy the Fount because it was the type of order he would be likely to follow. She did so to set him and Leroy on a collision course so Leroy can defeat him, which is the task she has set for Leroy to accomplish for her.

The same night, Kitt dreamt of being submerged in the Fount of Inarieth. When she was underwater, she could see two silhouettes standing beside the water, but when he was above water, they were nowhere to be seen, though the surroundings appeared to be those of long ago. She also felt strange feelings that were not originating from her. She intuited that they were coming from one of the figures on the shore and were those of love and fear, specifically fear of betrayal by some third entity. This figure wanted some sort of contingency plan in place.

When Kitt focused more on this feeling, the bottom of the pool cracked and sucked her down into a black void. Eventually, she landed on a surface that looked like black marble with veins of red. She tried to reach out with her mind and communicate with a mysterious presence that was there. When she turned her thoughts to different people and organizations, she felt a sense of connection to herself, Flynt, and Ziolda as well as angry reactions to Oswald, Leroy, and the Blood of Vol, and a very angry, violent reaction to Lord Soth.

In the morning, you saw the ship that was anchored in the Tempest Strait start to sail away. Kitt saw a dark, swirling mass near the helm for a split second, which looked like the portal through which Lord Soth had vanished. Leroy recognized the ship as the Liberation, owned by Captain Shadow. And Ziolda felt her pendant burn as if with anger and felt a thought, not her own, directed at the ship saying, "You dared again, you failed again."

When you reached Izzy and the Wandering Inn, she played a song she had composed while you were gone, inspired by Leroy's story. The Wandering Inn took you to Inarion, where Anna stayed to start rebuilding, then you headed north to rendezvous with Hulrik and his airship, The Kestrel. Hulrik flew you to Regalport and filled you in on some details of the city and of the Lhazaar Principalities as a whole. He dropped you off and then flew away. He didn't want to keep his ship there because there are so many pirates. Izzy came as well, with the Wandering Inn attached to the bottom of The Kestrel. It detached with you all in it and cannonballed into the ocean outside of the city.

After your not-so-subtle entrance, you made your way around the city after Izzy helped secure lodgings at the local Gold Dragon Inn. Kitt sold the dwarven mithral crown for an obscene amount of money, which you used to buy some new gear and get tattoos. On a notice board you saw posters for six escaped prisoners. You recognized Shadow and the name of Alkes Rigel, the astromancer from Kitt's college who had been studying the myth of Draksholm Island.

After spending the day shopping, you waited until evening and went to the local tavern, Whistler's Whirlpool. Inside, you could just see the eponymous whirlpool in a large basin in the floor. Above the whirlpool is a square balcony where people upstairs can look down at it.

As you scanned the patrons from the doorway, you saw many interesting-looking people standing out from the average tavern-goers. There was a table of hardened sailors playing cards. One of them was an elf with no sleeves, revealing the House Lyrandar dragonmark, the Mark of Storms, on his shoulder. Up on the balcony, a woman in a blue cloak overlooked everything below through a veil that covered the top half of her face as she leaned casually on the rail. In the far corner, two men sat in a booth. The smaller one with a weathered and salt-stained vest had a foot roguishly up on the table, one hand near the crossbow at his hip and the other holding his drink. The other was a massive, possibly part goliath, man with a crossbow that more resembles a small ballista. He was tearing messily into a giant turkey leg. Also on the bottom floor was a very stereotypically dressed pirate, complete with the parrot on his shoulder.

But the one that you took special notice of was a man in a brown cloak with the hood pulled up. He was sitting at the bar with a notepad by his drink. He was talking to the bartender and taking notes. You noticed that he does everything with his right hand, as his left looks like it was crushed at some point and healed badly. You immediately said, "That's our guy," and Ziolda went straight to the bar. She got one drink and then got a 5 on a CON save, so now she's immediately hammered.

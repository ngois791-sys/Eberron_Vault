Arc 8 — Forged by War

Sessions 46–47

Session 46 Session 47

Session 46

Last time, you entered the mists of the Mournlands. You landed on the shores north of Eston and pushed your boat out into the river so it would drift away and not be found by the warforged. You then began heading south to get the lay of the land.

Immediately, you spotted a massive warforged in the sky far off in the distance, and you barely managed to hide before it could spot you. You sent your scouts, Tallara and Fenlin, to get a look at the city of Eston, and they reported back that there appeared to be a society developing there around the factories that make the Fabrication-class warforged.

Desi told you that the Fabrication-class warforged were the first ones ever made, and that they were made in Eston. Back then, they were called the Steelbound. Later, the technology was used to make Warrior-class soldiers, and they were all renamed warforged.

You continued south and saw that the warforged are building extensions for the lightning rail lines that will connect Eston and Kalazart as well as The Clockwork Keep in the mountains to the east. You also came across the remnants of some battlefields and Cyrans who were killed in the Mourning. While Ziolda and the Thrane soldiers respectfully bowed their heads, the others looted the bodies. You were able to pick up a little bit of gold and a couple of magical items. Kitt found some Scale Mail of Gleaming that she asked Desi to rework into armor for Peanut, and Leroy found an Ioun Stone of Protection that he gave to Kitt.

Your scouts reported that the western border, near Breland and Aundair had wall defenses with ballistae and some form of weaponry on the top of two tall towers.

You continued down the western side of the Glass Plateau and sent the druids along with Fenlin to fly up to look at the city of Making. Unlike Eston, the city has been entirely converted to factories producing Warrior-class warforged. There was no colossus actively being made, but the infrastructure is there to do it. Kitt looked around for how they were moving the mined Zaverys dragonshards up to the top of the plateau and couldn't find any obvious way. They don't appear to be using airships at all, though they are building a lightning rail line to more easily traverse the obsidian glass. This led Kitt to surmise that they must be moving the dragonshards up through the plateau directly from the mines through some sort of vertical shaft.

When the scouts returned, you continued southward into the Field of Ruins, where warforged heavily patrol the border, looking for scavengers who venture into the mists. You encountered one of these patrols and destroyed them, then you dumped the bodies into one of the pit traps you had fallen into.

While the bulk of your band found a sort of staging area where prisoners are held before being moved to the mines, Kitt went with the scouts to try to get a glimpse of The Cloak, who is in charge of the patrols in the Field of Ruins. The group rolled insanely high with the help of Kitt's Pass Without Trace, and they were able to get very close. Kitt saw that whereas many of the warforged had relatively featureless plates on their faces, The Cloak had one that was a little more decorative under the hood of their eponymous cloak.

You continued to the southern end of The Glass Plateau and took a detour to visit the den that Leroy and Ronin had previously tracked the werewolves to. Inside, you found that all of the werewolves had been violently killed some time ago. Investigation revealed that they were killed by some sort of slashing weapon that was powerful enough to get through their hides.

You then went to Leroy's old home and found that the headstones where Leroy buried his wife and daughter were relatively well-tended, while the marker Leroy put up for Arel seemed to be ignored. When Kitt detected no smell of any werewolves visiting the scene, Leroy started to get some hope that Arel wasn't turned after all. He left a note on Arel's grave marker that said,

"Son, if you read this, I'm sorry I never found you. Head to The Silver Wood, mention my name, I'll find you soon enough."

Then Leroy declared that you were going to burn the whole city of Making to the ground.

Session 47

Last time, you made your way north from Leroy's farm toward the Zaverys dragonshard mines. Along the way you came across the remains of an abandoned village where Leroy used to do some trading as a farmer. Searching through the ruins, you found evidence that some of the buildings had collapsed because of earthquakes, presumably caused by the Mourning. There were also little growths of obsidian that had tiny flecks of red. Ronin told you that the mines get larger Zaverys dragonshards out of the stone of the plateau that had been transmuted to obsidian glass. These little flecks were very tiny Zaverys dragonshard fragments, but not large or powerful enough to be used.

Further searching through the ruins led to Kitt finding an Orb of Remembrance that contained an image of the market square like a photograph on someone's desk. The orb is capable of storing an image of its surroundings and can be overwritten. Kitt sniffed around the village and got the sense that a human had been there long since the Mourning, but not for a while. There were also no signs of warforged, likely because there was not much valuable salvage in the village.

You pressed on and encountered three large warforged with huge picks. You tried to follow them, but they eventually noticed you and attacked, assuming you to be escaped miners. Each of the warforged detonated when killed and you gathered the pieces as best you could to inspect them. You found that the technology inside them had advanced since your friend Desi was made. Being one of the earliest models of the Steelbound, she is powered by Khyber dragonshards that have to be replaced to keep her operating. These warforged had more efficient cables of Khyber dragonshards inside some sort of polymer that acted as a nervous system connected to the Zaverys dragonshard that supplied the power.

You did your best to hide the ruin, or at least disguise your number, though the grassland is still very charred from the flames and explosions. Then you continued to a point where you could overlook the southernmost of the mines. You saw an entrance into the tunnels through the obsidian, and a nearby stockpile of crates in a fenced area. At the entrance to the area was one of the huge warforged you had seen flying when you first entered the Mournlands. It was completely motionless, just staring outward from the fenced area into the camp.

There were a few warforged walking about and another walled, fenced area where miners were sleeping. You spotted that some sort of metal bands lined the fifteen-foot-high walls around them and there were roughly twenty people inside.

You sent Nia, Fenlin, and Ronin in to scout, with Kitt hiding in Ronin's pocket as a bat. While Nia and Fenlin kept a lookout, Ronin and Kitt approached the wall around the miners. You found that the metal bands had a hexagonal pattern on them, and Ronin, with his storm sorcery, could sense the electricity in them.

Kitt flew over the wall and turned back into a halfling, putting her hand over the mouth of an older, sleeping goblin and started speaking into his mind. She told him that you were going to help the miners escape, and he seemed skeptical that you could pull it off and nervous about the consequences of trying. He informed you that he's the de facto foreman of this crew, and that there are two other crews that operate in shifts: one resting, one mining, and one transporting at any given time.

When Kitt mentioned escaping, the goblin's eyes darted toward a sleeping man, and Kitt moved on to speak with him. The man introduced himself as Ander and looked excited by the prospect of escape. Kitt told him that Ronin could deactivate the shock collars, but you decided to wait on that to prevent any warforged from discovering your plans too early. Ander told you that the warforged try to keep them unaware of what's going on and the shapes of the mines, but Ander has been building a mental map and taking notes of everything he can, in preparation for some sort of escape. One of the major hurdles had been how to get around the shock collars.

Kitt told Ander to quietly spread the word through the miners and that you would try to start the escape the next day, or the next night at the latest.

Turning into a bat again, Kitt left but stopped to pick up a pebble and place it on top of the wall, where the largest metal band is. It depressed one of the hexagons, but nothing else seemed to happen. Kitt didn't investigate it further, and the scouting team headed back out of the camp.

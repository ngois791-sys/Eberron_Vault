The Draconic Prophecy

Cryptic visions of the seers of Draksholm — of three Champions, and the darkness they must face.

  • Category Prophecy

History Prophecy

The four greatest seers of Draksholm put forth all their combined power to receive visions of a future even the gods couldn't foresee. Some received visions. Some simply received a powerful sense of things that would become true, which they struggled to put into words. Some felt it helpful to process the feelings by putting them to verse.

The result was interpretations that were cryptic and not nearly as clear as the seers had hoped. The interpretations were recorded on stone tablets and set into one of the walls of the Temple of the Progenitors on Draksholm. Over time, interest in deciphering them waned, and they became nothing more than interesting relics in a museum.

One of the tablets was taken by the dragonborn leader Cuprir during the destruction of Draksholm. She brought it with her across the seas to Khorvaire, and it was later guarded by the dragonborns' descendants, the kobold Dragonmonks of Q'barra. This tablet read:

Arise, o Sun, and herald the day
A guide to be, a light on the Way
Arise, o Sun, and light up the sky
Else hope shall wither, shrivel, and die
Be free, o Sun, and unleash your wrath
On those who turn and stray from the Path

Arise, o Stars, with heavenly shine
Dispel the dark, this duty is thine
Arise, o Stars, the guide of the night
Let none discount or question your might
Take heart, o Stars, when all seems in vain
For thou art one, of light split in twain

O Sun, o Stars, shine bright now as one
Through day, through night, 'til darkness is done

Shine out, o Guides, for champions rise
And turn now east, to lights in thine eyes
Mingle thine light, to show them the way
And give council, when choices they weigh

For now comes time, for evil to fall
The Light, as one, must answer the call
The Stars, the Map, will follow the shards
The Sun, the Flare, who shields and who guards
Champions three, yet company five
At Regalport, Guides, soon shall arrive

The other three tablets were taken by Alderdia into the Great Diamond when he shifted the island into a demiplane. His powerful act of magic wiped knowledge of these tablets from all memory, and made it so that only the three Champions could gain the knowledge by entering the Great Diamond. One tablet was stored in each of the planes within the Great Diamond, each associated with one of the Progenitor Dragons. Once the challenge was passed, the Champion of the associated god received the knowledge of the prophecy.

The Tablet of Siberys

Shattered above the world
Great Siberys resides
While war fractures the Land
Cunning Darkness divides

Graced with silvery flames
His Chosen One shall rise
Reclaimed from Death's fell grip
Silver-ringed be her eyes

Not wholly of this world
Though its guardian, blessed
For Khyber she shall ride
Siberys at her chest

Bound to warriors' souls

The Tablet of Eberron

I beheld the fires of war burning high over the land. The flames cast long shadows that obscured much from my sight. But I knew that dark things lurked and grew in those shadows, for such is the way of the most cunning and patient of evils.

Then, bursting forth from the ruins, a great wave of grey. The fires sputtered, but the embers remained, and dark clouds loomed over the world. For a time, there was peace. But peace is a lie. A feint. Always, forces are moving in the dark. Ever seeking power. Ever seeking dominion over those they quietly despise.

Many moons passed. Several long years seemed but a blink to my eyes. And always, the threat of war grew anew. Until a child, born of two Houses and bearing a mark long since vanished, came into a new power. The child grew and learned this ancient, forgotten power, drawing on it to avert terrible disaster and usher in an age of true peace.

Shrouded in those who fell to the dark things, victims of everlasting spite, and wielding the strength of Eberron's children, the champion of the very World stood before the Darkness. The stars fell from the sky and the ground burst open with tongues of fire.

But when the sun rose again, and the clouds were scattered, there were no shadows where dark things could hide. Leaderless and vulnerable, the dark things were vanquished, and all fell blissfully quiet for a hundred blinks of my watchful eyes.

The Tablet of Khyber

Thrice cursed shall he be. Bereft of kin and expelled from his lands, he shall ever wander in search of vengeance. Though he give his blood and the blood of many foes, it shall never slake his thirst.

Chosen by the Dragon shall he be. And the Dragon he will choose. Desperation shall drive him, and at the utmost of need, he shall feel the might of the Great Wyrm raise him up. He would be her champion. Her blade in the darkness. Her instrument of revenge.

Yet stubborn shall he be. And she shall discount his will. For though her machinations seek to wreak ruin through him, the ruin shall fall upon herself. Wyrm shall turn against wyrm once more. The lands shall buckle and break. The heavens shall descend. The dead shall seek her heart. The ancient powers shall grind her to dust. And her champion shall free himself, and all peoples, of her shadow forever.

A Prophecy of the Cult of the Dragon Below

Later, the party encountered a separate prophecy — written in Khorvaire, not Draksholm — in the possession of the Cult of the Dragon Below:

Son of Stone and Scion of Stars
From ancient slumber, now awake
To burn the world and cleanse its scars
Avenge thine father, slay the snake

A flaming titan of the skies
Clad in armor of darkest night
A god of fire in lizard's guise
Savior, destroyer, peerless might

With cursed roars and azure fire
With flash of steel and lightning's crack
With shroud of death and spirits' ire
Arise, now, Zaverys the Black

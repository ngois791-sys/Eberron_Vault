Visions in the Great Diamond

What each Champion witnessed on passing through the gate.

  • Category Prophecy

Prophecy

Siberys Domain

Ziolda, you are the first to pass through the gate. As the wall of the diamond closes behind you, you find yourself surrounded by an ever-shifting crystalline substance. A gentle force pushes at your back, as if ushering you forward. Glints of refracted light sparkle all around you; the effect is almost dizzying. Soon, the colors around you change, resolving into tiny images that last only a fraction of a second as you push through the diamond.

A flash of blue-white lightning across a sea of dark clouds; a swirling, faintly yellow mass in inky blackness; the splash of flames across a burnished shield; a massive explosion flattening humans and trees alike; the empty eye socket of a dragon burning with indigo flames; three figures dressed in ivory robes under gleaming silver armor, scimitars at their hips; an empty suit of armor being thrown from a mountainside; and a shimmering pool of molten silver.

Then you feel the pressure lift as the world resolves into a vast, black expanse dotted with stars. The path below your feet shines with a pale, white light and supports you perfectly, despite its misty, ethereal quality. It extends further into the expanse toward a ring of light in the distance. Around the ring are intricate patterns and shapes woven from the same glowing substance. Looking up and down, you see more paths like this one, along with similar rings of light.

Leroy, you are second to pass through the gate. As the wall of the diamond closes behind you, you find yourself in a thicket of needle-like, crystalline shards. No matter which way you look, there seems to be one poised to take out your eye. As you move forward, they press against you — just enough to feel the sharp, threatening pressure, but not enough to pierce your skin. Soon your eyes adjust to see beyond the points mere millimeters away, and you catch flashes of images in the crystals.

A curved hunting knife with engraved script down the blade; a drop of blood falling into a rune-etched diamond; silvery hands holding a dragon's egg that twitches and shakes violently; a gout of blood as a werewolf slashes with its claws; a figure collapsing into the snow on a windswept mountainside overlooking a perfectly still lake; an all-consuming blast of fire; a hulking orc standing, arms raised, before a glowing, molten crater; and a blurry, jagged tower silhouetted against a sky of swirling, luminous mist.

Then you feel the needles lift from your skin and you emerge next to Ziolda in the void.

Kitt, you are the last to pass through the gate. As the wall of the diamond closes behind you, you find yourself surrounded by a substance that sparkles like crystals but is otherwise as smooth and clear as water. As you move forward, you slowly become aware of a pressure on your right hand, as if someone were placing their hand in yours — but when you look down, you see nothing there. The invisible hand raises yours ever so slightly as it guides you onward, and you begin to catch wavering images through your fluid surroundings.

A man with pointed ears looking up in wonder at a floating, warbling mass of luminous fluid; a cloaked man with a crimson blindfold holding out a bloody hand toward a stirring corpse; a gargantuan tree overlooking a sprawling forest; two halflings dancing in the light of a fiery hearth; a once-fine mansion ruined by time and the elements; the silhouettes of two dragons circling each other in the air, gouts of flame and shadow spewing from their mouths and colliding; a choked and diseased-looking landscape with the skeletons of trees crumbling to dust; and a circle of earth collapsing to a pile of massive bones.

Then you feel the invisible hand drop yours and you emerge next to Ziolda and Leroy.

Eberron Domain

Leroy, you see the trunks of the great trees around you start turning to stone. Gradually, they form into large columns holding up the ceiling of a great hall. You tap your fingers on the arm of your throne as you gaze out across the hall toward the large double doors on the far wall.

Flanking the hall are a dozen warforged. Each wears a pale grey, cowled cloak over their steel body, draped over one shoulder while the other arm holds a pike that glimmers with crackling energy. They turn their heads as one toward the double doors as the doors begin to creak open.

Two more humanoid warforged enter, alongside a massive warforged tiger. In the hall beyond, you can see a small company of warforged warriors, under the command of these two, waiting patiently for your audience with them to be done.

The larger of the two, for whom the tiger was clearly constructed to be a mount, would stand head and shoulders over most people despite its slightly hunched appearance. It carries no obvious weapons, but you can see the tips of two extendable blades peeking out from its forearms, just above the wrist. It leads the trio to the foot of your dais, sweeps back its long black cloak, and drops to one knee, placing a fist over its chest as it bows its head respectfully.

The smaller warforged, standing a couple of paces back, follows suit. This one has roughly normal humanoid proportions and wears a maroon cowl with the hood pulled up over its armored head. Even the tiger bows, its overlapping bronze plates shifting smoothly over the many pistons and mechanical articulations visible beneath.

You raise your arms welcomingly toward your servants. Your generals. Your creations. Your loyal Forge-Sworn.

The sleeves of your opulent maroon robe slide up to reveal black metal hands with gold glinting from within the joints. The robe, dyed from its original green and gold, was taken from the belongings of your land's former rulers — those too weak to survive in the land anymore, who had been determined to see your subjects as nothing more than tools to fight their war. They had never seen them as sentient beings. But now the Forge-Sworn Empire stands proud, and it will soon rise up and take revenge against the rest of Khorvaire.

As you stand, so do your subjects.

Near the bottom of your field of vision, something stirs in the shadows. A large object — a tail, dark as the night — had lain motionless, wrapped around your throne. But now it slowly slides around, and a huge shadow stretches across the hall as the being behind the throne stands.

You stride down from the dais and toward the larger warforged. But as you reach it, it morphs into Ziolda, the smaller warforged shrinks into Kitt, and the world shifts back to the forest floor.

Kitt, you start to feel dizzy and fall to your knees in the damp moss. Only, it's not moss anymore. It's a flagstone floor in a moonlit hall. The hall looks like it was once ornate, but has slowly gone to ruin. Tall pillars support the high ceiling, and a flurry of snow, glinting in the moonlight, drifts in through a half-broken stained glass window high on the wall.

You look down as you kneel in the center of the room and reach for a dagger resting on the floor before you. As you do, you see that your arms are extremely pale. The moment you pull the dagger from its sheath and turn your arms over, you see that the undersides of both are heavily scarred with angry red slashes across your wrists.

This time will be different. It has to be. You grasp the dagger with both hands and turn the shaking point toward your chest. You take a deep breath, steeling yourself, and ram the dagger into your heart. You double over from the pain as blood starts to ooze from the wound and trickle down your hands.

Not enough. The dagger is still blocking it. You yank the dagger free. But the gush of blood you expected doesn't come. The pain starts to recede, and you feel the place where the knife was. It's nothing but a puckered scar, already knit back together.

In a fit of rage, you hurl the dagger across the room. As you do, you notice the ornate silver band around your upper arm. The sapphire set in it flashes, and the dagger strikes one of the stone pillars, a small explosion blowing a chunk away from the stone where it struck.

The violent motion loosened your band ever so slightly, and as it slips down your arm, you see the edge of a distinctive, burn-like dragonmark underneath.

At the end of the hall, a man — apparently a guard, from the look of his armor — runs into the room, probably alerted by the sound of the breaking pillar. He wears a tattered red and black tabard over rusty plate. As he gets closer, you see that he's clearly undead: his skin is drawn tightly across his skull, with sunken cheeks and eyes. He makes a move to help you up before morphing into Leroy.

Ziolda, the world around you shifts. The darkness of the jungle floor becomes the darkness of a swiftly approaching night, and the surrounding trees become a mountainous horizon with the setting sun just visible over the peaks.

You find yourself standing on the parapet of a fort built among these mountains, spanning a narrow valley. Deep in the distance to the west, sunlight illuminates a horde approaching — hundreds of skittering creatures storming up the path to the fort.

You look to your left and see a row of heavily armored orc archers setting arrows to their strings. You look to your right and see a tall, dark-skinned man looking nervously at the approaching horde. In contrast with the orcs, he carries only an oak staff and wears simple robes.

You raise your gauntleted hand and clap him on the shoulder reassuringly. He turns from the sight and gives you a determined nod. Putting a hand to his neck, he places his fingers on a pendant that flares to life with violet light, and his robes are instantly transmuted into flexible tree bark with the color and strength of steel.

You raise your halberd and plant it firmly on the parapet. As you do, the blade starts to glow gold. You share another nod with the druid, and you both turn to march down to the gate. The two of you step out onto the path together, and the doors close behind you as the archers draw back their arrows.

They unleash the first volley as the monsters scramble into range. The druid raises his staff to the sky and clouds begin to swirl overhead, sending bolts of violet lightning into the horde, vaporizing them in clusters. He then slams the butt of his staff into the ground, and the earth shakes violently beneath the monsters. Great fissures swallow many whole; others are claimed by falling boulders.

You look at the druid again and give a “Shall we?” gesture toward the horde. The druid smirks, and the two of you begin sprinting toward the monsters. Your golden halberd is a blur, cutting down monsters before they can even get close. Arrows arc over your head to strike deeper into the morass as you send groups flying back with bursts of light.

But there are so many that you're forced to give ground. You lose sight of the druid among the flailing limbs and snapping teeth. You hold out your hand and stop one monster midair as it leaps toward you. But another shoves it aside and smashes the head off your halberd, leaving you with just the broken haft. The light of the blade fades as it spins off into a chasm, but you keep fighting with the remaining haft.

You point the haft at the one who broke it, and its eyes go blank before it turns and starts attacking its fellows. Your attacks become a rapid spinning of the staff, pointing each end in turn at a monster to blast it backward with invisible force. As you do, the staff starts to glow with an icy light.

When it seems like you will be overrun, you raise the staff overhead and bring it crashing down on the ground with a massive wave of energy. A wall of stone, extending as far as the eye can see on either side of you, rockets up from the ground, ending the attack and creating an impenetrable barrier.

You lean heavily on the broken haft, exhausted. You see the druid kneeling nearby, staring up at the newly formed wall in awe. Then you realize that you're back on the forest floor, and the druid before you is not the tall man, but Kitt.

Khyber Domain

Each of the party members encountered objects that were only visible or responsive to them:

  • When the party entered the gravity caves at the beginning of Khyber's domain, there were claw marks on the marble that changed the gravity when touched. To Kitt and Leroy, they appeared as gouges in the stone, but to Ziolda they appeared filled with a silvery metal flecked with gold. When Ziolda touched the marks, they emitted a faint, golden mist.
  • In the caves of mirrors, the party encountered pools of luminous, blue crystals in a constant state of flux between liquid and solid. Kitt found a gem at the bottom of her pool that was the key to open the door forward. When she touched it, it pulsed with a lavender light that illuminated the roiling purple smoke inside, and it radiated heat like a living being. These effects did not appear for Ziolda or Leroy.
  • In the riddle chamber near the end of the Khyber domain, Ziolda and Kitt saw the floor as solid stone carved with weblike patterns, while Leroy saw a mesh of deep blue, woven thread through which he could see the next room below.

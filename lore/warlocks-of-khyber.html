Divine Agents Villains

Lord Soth

  • True Name Unknown

A man, race unknown, obscured by heavy armor and a masked helm with glowing blue eyes.

According to Izzy, the name “Lord Soth” comes from fantasy and fairy tales told in Sharn. People create their own stories and adventures, and the heroes always change, but Lord Soth is a common villain across most of them. In the usual tale, he was a brave and respected knight of noble birth during a time of war. Forbidden by his king and the royal council from pursuing a powerful talisman that would help end the war — they didn't trust the strange magic on it — he defied them and sought it out. Its power helped him lead his people to victory, but it also cursed him, slowly corrupting him into an evil and twisted version of his former self.

Lord Soth is the leader of the Blood of Vol, the greatest enemies of the Church of the Silver Flame. He infiltrated the Council of Cardinals with the aid of Elar d'Thuranni and Lhazaar smugglers, using one of Elar's asters to create a portal on an inkwell brought into the council chambers. He assassinated Cardinal Halidor and disappeared into smoke, but Jaela was able to resurrect Halidor shortly afterward.

The party first encountered Lord Soth at the Fount of Inarieth, where he and his fellow cultists killed the members of the Order of the Emerald Claw the party was pursuing. They then attempted a ritual to destroy the Fount of Inarieth, but were thwarted by the party; Lord Soth made his escape through a portal spell.

Khyber told Leroy that Lord Soth has become powerful enough that her sway over him is decreasing, so she told Leroy to kill him. She instructed Lord Soth to destroy the Fount of Inarieth because she knew it would appeal to him and put him on a collision course with Leroy.

The Umbral Shade

  • True Name Valdren
  • Race Valenar elf man

The first werewolf and leader of the Dark Pack, Valdren made his pact with Khyber to become a werewolf and to quietly gain power by building his pack in the shadows and infiltrating organizations across Khorvaire.

Valdren was killed by Tira Miron in the crusade against the Dark Pack centuries ago. The remnants of the Dark Pack went into hiding and brought Valdren's spirit back centuries later.

Valdren's spirit was killed by Leroy after Leroy made his own pact with Khyber, in the final battle to finish off the Dark Pack.

The Great Demon

  • True Name Unknown

A man. Legends call him a demon, but art found in Flamekeep depicts him as a massively oversized orc — as did Leroy's vision of him in the Great Diamond.

The Great Demon managed to bypass the Labyrinth separating the Demon Wastes from the rest of Khorvaire and invaded Thrane with an army of monsters. Tira Miron was recalled from the crusade against House Vol to defeat him. She died in the process, and the city of Flamekeep was built around the site of their battle.

After defeating the pirates in the Lhazaar Principalities, Leroy found that one of them was essentially a sub-warlock of the Great Demon, suggesting that he has returned in the Demon Wastes.

The Lord of Dust

  • True Name Festarik
  • Race Dragonborn man

Once the ruler of Draksholm, Festarik was deeply interested in the Draconic Prophecy and eventually became involved in experiments to improve the dragonborn race. He unknowingly made a pact with Khyber while accessing the arcane magic for his studies. This work resulted in the creation of the first draconians.

Khyber's magic overcame Festarik and used him and his draconians to destroy Draksholm and the dragonborn. Festarik's mate, Cuprir, subdued him and put him in a stasis chamber, intending to help him later. But the destruction forced Cuprir to take part of the Draconic Prophecy and flee, leading the surviving dragonborn to Khorvaire while Alderdia pulled the remnants of Draksholm into a pocket plane.

The knowledge of how to create draconians survived among the dragonborn who escaped, and eventually made its way to the Cult of the Dragon Below.

The party discovered Festarik in his stasis chamber and freed him. While they were discussing matters with Alderdia in the Great Diamond, Festarik was overcome by Khyber's magic and possessed Izzy, Alkes, and Kassandra to attack the party.

When he was defeated, he asked Leroy to try to remove Khyber's magic from him. Leroy failed, and Festarik requested an honorable death instead, so he couldn't bring any more harm.

The Lord of Blades

  • True Name Unknown
  • Race Warforged

The Lord of Blades emerged from the Mourning to lead the warforged that survived the cataclysm. He gave the survivors a choice: be part of his Forge-Sworn Empire, or be exiled from the lands that formerly made up Cyre.

He seemingly wants to create a nation only for the warforged, whom he views as exploited by the Cyrans in the Last War — the first warforged were actually called the Steelbound and were not intended for war until Cyran leaders saw their usefulness as soldiers. His ambitions beyond this nation are unclear; he has not indicated plans to invade neighboring countries, but he has set up powerful artillery on the border and rebuilt the factories that make various martial classes of warforged, including the warforged colossi.

He is aware of the powerful obsidian dragonshards beneath the Glass Plateau and has enslaved humanoid “invaders” to mine for them. He has also greatly improved warforged technology in recent years.

He has constructed the Clockwork Keep, which spans the Glowing Chasm in the north, as his headquarters, while his top lieutenants — known as the Cloak and the Titan — do his bidding in the land to the south.

Suspected Warlock Connections

  • Leroy saw a vision in the Great Diamond of a figure collapsing on a snowy mountainside. The party suspects this may be one of the warlocks.
  • Kitt saw a vision in the Great Diamond through the eyes of a woman with a Mark of Death on her arm and scars on her wrists. She drove a knife into her heart but flung it across the room in anger when it had no effect on her. The party suspects this may be one of the warlocks.
  • Kitt has had multiple visions of an old man who claimed to exist to spite Eberron, tearing everything away from Eberron's druids to torment them. He claimed responsibility for the disease that killed Kitt's parents, though he had intended to kill Kitt, thinking her mother was Eberron's chosen. The party suspects this may be one of the warlocks.
  • The fate of Traelor is unknown after he turned from being a paladin of Siberys to a warlock of Khyber. He used Khyber's power to create the Labyrinth and later turned on the archdruid Oalian. Traelor disappeared after winning that battle, as did Oalian's spellbook.
  • The party found information from the Cult of the Dragon Below indicating they received the knowledge of how to create draconians from someone called the Nomad.
